From the Cape to Cambridgeshire

A legacy crossing continents

Origin Festival has held space in the Western Cape since 2010 — a New Year's gathering on Elandskloof Farm, deep in fynbos country. Now Regan and the Nano Records family bring the Origin stage to UK soil for the first time, in a forest sanctuary near Abbots Ripton.

Same ethos, same family, new biome. A weekend-long journey of conscious celebration where the bushveld spirit meets English oak. Expect deep psytrance journeys, twisted techno, dark progressive grooves and a live lounge for the slower moments.

"Uniting the tribes." — The Origin Way

SA speak

South African slang · lingo · the vibe
Open the glossary ▼
Howzit
Casual greeting. "How are you?" but more relaxed. Response: "Good, hey."
Lekker
Nice, good, great. "That set was lekker" = that DJ was brilliant.
Just now
Not immediately. "I'll see you just now" = sometime later, not right now. Can be minutes or hours.
Eish
Expression of frustration, surprise, or exasperation. "Eish, the tent blew away!"
Ag
Expression of dismissal or mild frustration. "Ag, it's fine" = don't worry about it.
Braai
Barbecue. A gathering around a fire with meat and friends. Sacred ritual. "Let's braai tomorrow."
Ubuntu
"I am because we are." The philosophy of shared humanity. Origin's core ethos.
Sawubona
Zulu greeting meaning "I see you." Acknowledgment of another's presence and worth.
Ayoba
That's excellent, awesome, cool. "Your outfit is ayoba!"
Shame
Not embarrassment — expression of endearment or sympathy. "Shame, you look tired" = I care about you.
Jol
Party, good time. "We're going to jol this weekend" = we're going to have a great time.
Is it
Tag question for agreement. "Nice day, is it?" = you agree it's a nice day, yeah?
Hectic
Intense, crazy, wild. "That rave was hectic" = absolutely mad in the best way.
Sho't left
Just a moment, wait. "Sho't left, I'm coming" = give me a second.
Fynbos
Unique Cape vegetation. Where Origin Festival SA was born. The spirit we're bringing north.
